DCM methylation

  • Occurs once in 512 bp (if random)
    • (in reality, once in ~300 bp, occurs more often in gene-rich areas)
  • Copied during cell division (~80% of DCM marks)
  • Does not affect biological processes in mammalian cells.

And what about expression?

Binomial?

  • Assume that in cell $i$ any DCM site can always get methylated with the probability $p_{i}$.
  • If there is no signal in gene $j$: $$ M_{ij} \sim \text{Binomial}(N_{ij}, p_{i}) $$
  • Any extra methylation is due to past transcription.

$$D_{ij} = \text{sign}(\frac{M_{ij}}{N_{ij}} - p_{i}) \sqrt{2 \left( \log L(M_{ij} | N_{ij}, \frac{M_{ij}}{N_{ij}}) - \log L(M_{ij} | N_{ij}, p_{i}) \right)}$$
